# Chinese Five-Spice Almond Cookies

**Source**: Originally from a Betty Crocker grocery store recipe card, I've adapted this recipe to use a homemade sugar cookie base instead of a premade mix as the original recipe required.
---

## Ingredients

- 1-1/2 c. Powdered Sugar
- 1 c. Butter, softened
- 1 Egg
- 1 tsp. Vanilla
- 1/2 tsp. Almond Extract
- 1/2 c. Sliced Almonds
- 2-3 tsp. Chinese Five-Spice Powder
- 2-1/2 c. Flour
- 1 tsp. Baking Soda
- 1 tsp. Cream of Tartar
- Coarse White Sparkling Sugar

## Directions

- Cream together butter, egg, powdered sugar, vanilla, and almond extract. Mix in flour, baking soda, chinese five-spice, almonds, and cream of tarter. Cover and refrigerate at least 2 hours.
- Heat oven to 375°F. Either roll into 1-inch balls and dip one side in sugar OR roll dough out to 3/16-inch thick to cut into shapes. Place on baking sheet (balls with sugar side up). Sprinkle cut-out shapes with coarse sugar. Bake 7-8 minutes.
